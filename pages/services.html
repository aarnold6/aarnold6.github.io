What Jacquelynn Offers

CFT Services

All sessions are rooted in the Gillespie Approach — gentle, hands-on CFT tailored to each client's age, history, and needs.

Sessions

What to Expect

Craniosacral Fascial Therapy is effective across all ages. Each session is individualized — no two bodies unwind the same way.

🌱 Newborns & Infants

Infant CFT Session

Infant sessions are gentle, nurturing, and performed while the baby is held, lying on a comfortable surface, or nursing — whatever keeps them most at ease. Jacquelynn uses an extremely light touch to assess and address fascial strain patterns that may have developed during pregnancy or birth.

Many parents notice changes in feeding, sleep, comfort, and temperament within a session or shortly after. CFT is safe for newborns and can be started as early as the first days of life.

Reflux & colic Oral ties Torticollis Feeding difficulties Birth trauma Plagiocephaly Sleep support
🌿 Children

Children's CFT Session

Children's sessions are adapted to be calm, comfortable, and engaging. Jacquelynn works with the child's natural rhythm and comfort level. Sessions for children address accumulated strain from birth, falls, illness, and the physical and emotional demands of growing up.

CFT can support children through developmental milestones, behavioral challenges, and physical discomfort — complementing, not replacing, other forms of care.

Developmental delays ADHD / ADD Sleep issues Anxiety Sensory sensitivities Headaches Nervous system support
🌳 Adults

Adult CFT Session

Adult sessions are performed fully clothed on a comfortable table. Jacquelynn listens to the body's craniosacral fascial rhythm and follows the tissues as they unwind — addressing layers of strain that may have accumulated over years or decades.

Sessions are deeply relaxing and often leave clients feeling lighter, more mobile, and better regulated. The number of sessions needed varies by individual, and Jacquelynn will discuss this with you after your first visit.

Chronic pain Migraines TMJ / jaw pain Anxiety & depression Pelvic pain Trauma recovery Nervous system regulation

Before Your Visit

What to Know & Bring

A little preparation helps make your first session as smooth as possible.

For Adult Sessions

  • Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing
  • Complete the first-visit intake form in advance if possible
  • Arrive a few minutes early to settle in
  • Avoid heavy meals immediately before the session
  • Bring a list of any current medications or supplements
  • Note any recent injuries, surgeries, or health changes

For Infant & Child Sessions

  • Bring your child's comfort item (pacifier, toy, blanket)
  • For infants: bring a change of clothes and feeding supplies
  • Schedule the session around a natural feeding/nap window
  • Complete the intake form for your child in advance
  • Note any birth history details you feel are relevant
  • A parent or guardian must be present for all minor sessions
