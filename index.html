Understanding the System

What is Fascia?

Fascia is the thin connective tissue that holds everything in your body together — and its health affects far more than most people realize.

The Fascial Web

Brain & Spinal Cord
Organs & Glands
Muscles & Tendons
Nerves & Blood Vessels
Bones & Joints

Fascia covers every structure in the body — forming one continuous, interconnected web from head to toe.

One Continuous
Connective Web

Fascia is a continuous layer of connective tissue that wraps around every structure in your body — muscles, bones, organs, nerves, and even the brain and spinal cord. Imagine a full-body web that connects every part of you, from your scalp to your feet, keeping everything working as one.

When the fascial system is healthy and unrestricted, cerebrospinal fluid moves freely throughout the brain, spinal cord, and body — supporting healthy cell function and clearing metabolic waste. When fascia tightens or becomes restricted — from birth, trauma, stress, or injury — that flow is disrupted, which can contribute to a wide range of physical and emotional symptoms.

✦   When fascia is free, your whole body can thrive

Who Can Benefit

CFT addresses fascial strain patterns that accumulate throughout life — from the moment of birth onward. Explore how it may benefit you or your loved one.

Newborns & Infants

Starting Life with Freedom to Move

Birth — even in the most straightforward cases — places significant strain on an infant's delicate craniosacral fascial system. Compression in the skull, neck, and body can create tension patterns that affect feeding, digestion, sleep, and overall comfort. CFT gently unwinds these strain patterns, giving your baby the free-moving start they deserve.

Conditions Often Addressed

Difficulty feeding at breast or bottle Whole body tension & tightness Nervous system dysregulation Reflux, colic & gas Constipation Oral ties: tongue & lip Torticollis Misshapen head / Plagiocephaly Birth trauma Sleep difficulties Physical & emotional trauma

Toddlers & School-Age Children

Supporting Development & Growth

As children grow, accumulated fascial tension can affect their development, behavior, and ability to thrive. From developmental milestones to focus and learning, the fascial system plays a crucial role. CFT provides gentle, child-friendly sessions that support healthy nervous system development and whole-body function.

Conditions Often Addressed
